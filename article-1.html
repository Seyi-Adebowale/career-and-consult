Must-Do as an employer during a job interview

  • PREPARE WELL

    I do find that there is a fair balance between preparation and seeing what happens naturally. Being prepared ahead allow you to make best hiring decisions.

    Pre-Interview Tips

    Keep to time; keeping to scheduled time boost their confidence in the job they are applying for, and if time or venue would change, candidates should be notified ahead

    Print out candidate’s resume; printing out candidate resume for review ahead of the interview is a good option for wise hiring process, allowing you to have a picture of the candidate before meeting them.

    Prepare a list of questions; a better way not to run out of questions with your candidate is to create a list of questions to ask them

  • Be Methodical

    Unstructured interview (that feels like a free-flowing conversation) can easily become non-job related

    During-interview Tips
    Choose questions carefully;

    to create a reliable assessment choices and to get precise answers to the questions you’ll be asking.

    Practice note taking;

    benefit of this tip can’t be over emphasized, it makes you pay attention, understand answers, also to remember answers later on.

    Rate candidate’s answer with consistent scale;

    rate every details; dressings, outspokenness, intelligence, body movement and so on.

  • Show You Care

    Open on a positive scale, ease them into the process, answer their questions politely, take your time.
